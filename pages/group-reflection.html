Individual Reflection

Samuel Ashton

What Went Well:
Overall, our group worked very well together. I believe that everyone contributed to the best of their ability. Some members may have felt that they had not contributed enough but brought this up in meetings themselves in order to be able to contribute more. Decisions on how we were going to proceed we made as a group and meeting minutes were taken to ensure that outcomes were met. We did not necessarily give each other deadlines until towards the end, everyone was of the understanding that tasks just had to be done.

What Could Be Improved:
The biggest struggle we had was communication as a whole group. While we used collaborative tools to our advantage, different schedules meant we were rarely able to meet as an entire group. I believe that this is possibly unique to an online study environment as a team project in the workforce would typically enforce certain work hours and therefore there would be more consistent availability.

What Was Surprising:
For me personally the most surprising thing was the motivation each member had to get on with their tasks to completion. Having worked as a leader of a Team in a business environment for many years, I am used to projects stalling when newer team members wait for approval of ideas before moving forward. With no corporate hierarchy all team members are equal which is refreshing!

What Have You Learnt About Groups:
My biggest learning from this exercise is how well a group can work when everyone’s motivation is the same. No matter what age, what prior experience we had, we are all aiming for the same goal and bringing what skills we have to the table. Without a company’s specific rules or policies (read red tape) to adhere to the team was able to get on with the job at hand however they saw fit.

Group Reflection

What Went Well:

Overall, our group worked very well together. Initially we were spread across different tools before agreeing on a single collaboration platform to work together on. The use of Microsoft OneNote and Office365 worked extremely well, allowing us to sort and categorise each part of the assignment, and then work together editing others work where necessary. We agreed on Roles early and each member was able to focus on their tasks, updating the team as they progressed.
We held regular meetings, documented in meeting minutes which we referred to in each following meeting. This allowed us to ensure that we were focused and had regular status updates. This also allowed members who may have been unable to attend every meeting a way to stay informed.

What Could Be Improved:

As a group we were able to identify one of our major weaknesses for this project, being a lack of communication particularly in the beginning phases. This could be due to time zone differences, current world events, or even prior work commitments. This lapse in communication early on resulted in some of the team feeling the assigning of tasks was delayed resulting in some members having trouble with deadlines.
While the team did try to distribute the work evenly, there was some opportunity to share the tasks more evenly throughout the team. By improving communication methods and working towards more collaboration in the team, the above matters would be negligible.

What Was Surprising:

Some of the things the we found surprising about our group was the diverse backgrounds and age ranges that we all come from, including locations around the world. Even this didn’t affect our ability to collaborate or cooperate. The group's ability to click and work well from the first meeting were quite surprising adding in everybody's crazy work schedule, it was impressive to see everyone organise themselves around their work and come together to put maximum effort into this assignment. The motivation of everyone in the group over the entire assessment period was impressive and keeping up a tempo like that was surprising.

What Have You Learnt About Groups:

The biggest lesson learnt during this group exercise is that communication is key. In an online environment where members have different commitments and even work in different time zones, communicating availability and progress is paramount. As a group everyone has learned it is important to make sure work is evenly distributed and that work commitments are known to everyone as so other members can potentially take on a bit more work to keep the team ahead of its own timeline.



Tools

Tools Used

  • Brackets
  • Discord
  • GitHub
  • Grammarly
  • Microsoft Teams
  • Microsoft OneNote
  • Microsoft Word
  • TeXworks

Brackets
Brackets was the program used to make the website, this program was chosen because it was a simple and easy to use text editor that was specifically made for web development.

Discord
Discord was used for communication at the start of the assignment, after some discussion, it was then agreed that all discussion will be moved to Microsoft Teams.

GitHub
GitHub was used for the groups Git repository and to host the group website, GitHub is easy to use and the GitHub pages feature makes it easy to host a website.

Grammarly
Grammarly was used to help with spell checking and sentence structures, after problems with nonsense suggestions, it was not used anymore.

Microsoft Teams
Microsoft Teams (Teams for short) was the main form of communication used for this assessment, Teams made it easy for group members to share files and the extra features, like the calendar, made it easy to plan meetings in advanced.

Microsoft OneNote
Microsoft OneNote (OneNote for short) was the main program used for writing, OneNote made it easy for group members to collaborate on a piece of writing. The tab and page features in OneNote made it easy to organize the writing sections in our assessment.

Microsoft Word
Microsoft Word (Word for short) was initially used for writing the report, after some discussion, it was agreed that the document would be written in LaTeX

TeXworks
TeXworks was used to write the document in LaTeX, it is a simple, multi-platform front-end program that makes writing in LaTeX easy.

Git Audit Trail

Our Git audit trail is not an accurate representation of the amount of work each member contributed. Each of the text sections were written in OneNote, not in GitHub. Cameron uploaded the group website to GitHub, but William was the one that created it, the website was shared in the groups Teams chat before the Git repository was made. William was in charge of adding the information to the website and PDF document, he was not the one that wrote all the text. The large amount of deletions are due to merging web pages together.

As a group we feel that this represents a typical workflow of a team producing a report. In this scenario GIT was used as a single version control system for the publishing of the document and Website, rather than a collaborative software development project, which GIT is more suited for. After we reviewed and edited content in OneNote as a group, having one person in charge of the final publishing ensured consistency of the end product.
